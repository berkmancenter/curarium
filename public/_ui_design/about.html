About Curarium Curarium leverages the power of the crowd to annotate, curate, & augment works with the aim of constructing shareable, media-rich stories & elaborate arguments about individual items as well as groups of items. Collections: Find and follow collections of interest to you OR ingest your own collection* of up to 30,000 records in just 5 minutes. Spotlights: Tell stories by pulling directly from the content you’ve collected in your trays. Publish, share, and export your spotlights. Explore: Within any collection, browse, filter, search, and generate visualizations. Annotate records and make additions to their metadata. Save individual records, groups of records, or visualizations to a tray. Categorize what you save using as many trays as you need. You: Start or join user circles to share and collaborate on work within a specified collection or set of collections. When you explore any collections in your circles, you can use a social tray to work in real time with circle members to collect and order records.

What is Curarium?
Curarium showcases the latent storytelling possibilities of collections using item-level annotations through top-level visualizations to bring both objects and the communities to which they belong into dialogue with one another.
Curarium Vocabulary
Annotation | Circle | Collection | Ingest | Record | Social Tray | Spotlight | Tray
How do I use Curarium?
Collect
  • Find and follow collections of interest to you
  • OR ingest your own collection* of up to 30,000 records in just 5 minutes. *Requires JSON files and offsite hosting
Explore
  • Within any collection, browse, filter, search, and generate visualizations.
  • Annotate records and make additions to their metadata.
  • Save individual records, groups of records, or visualizations to a tray.
  • Categorize what you save using as many trays as you need.
Spotlight
  • Tell stories by pulling directly from the content you’ve collected in your trays.
  • Publish, share, and export your spotlights.
User
  • Edit your collections, trays, and spotlights.
  • Track collections and users you follow.
  • Start or join user circles to share and collaborate on work within a specified collection or set of collections.
  • From your circle’s splash page, access circle-specific collections; check the newsfeed to find updated circle annotations, social trays, and spotlights; post comments to your circle members.
  • When you explore any collections in your circles, you can use a social tray to work in real time with circle members to collect and order records.
